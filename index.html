Acquisition criteria

Businesses we seek to acquire or partner with.

We consider majority acquisitions, recapitalizations, founder rollover equity, management partnerships, and selective minority structures when alignment is strong.

Company profile

Founder-led or family-owned operators with a strong reputation, recurring local demand, durable customer relationships, and a culture worth preserving.

Financial profile

Profitable companies with meaningful adjusted EBITDA, clean revenue quality, and a path to scalable operating systems.

Transaction types

Platform investments, add-on acquisitions, tuck-ins, succession solutions, and strategic mergers in fragmented markets.

M&A process

A clear path from introduction to close.

  1. 01

    Confidential introduction

    We learn the story, ownership goals, market position, team structure, culture, and what a successful outcome should protect.

  2. 02

    Preliminary review

    We evaluate normalized EBITDA, revenue mix, customer concentration, branch density, and platform or add-on fit.

  3. 03

    LOI and diligence

    A clear letter of intent sets valuation, structure, rollover equity, exclusivity, and the diligence plan before deeper work begins.

  4. 04

    Close and build

    We move through confirmatory diligence, definitive agreements, transition planning, and the first value creation priorities.

Investment thesis

Fragmented markets reward disciplined consolidation.

HVAC, plumbing, and mechanical services remain local, reputation-driven, and operationally intensive. That is exactly why thoughtful consolidation can matter.

Sterling Saxon looks for businesses where brand trust, technician quality, dispatch discipline, culture, and customer care can be strengthened without erasing what made the company valuable.
